# AI/BI Dashboards: Interactive Capabilities at a Glance

Nine capabilities that make AI/BI dashboards a serious analytics surface, with a healthcare lens.

## Cross-Filtering
What it is: click any chart and every visual filters to your selection, no SQL needed.
Use it for: trend analysis, root-cause investigation, and contextual drilldowns across visuals in real time.

## Drilling
What it is: move from summary to detail within the same dashboard.
Use it for: region to facility to provider, or year to month to day, without writing a new query.

## Forecasting and AI Functions
What it is: built-in ML and GenAI in SQL (ai_forecast, ai_query, ai_classify, ai_extract, ai_summarize).
Use it for: project encounter or readmission volume, classify or summarize text, all without leaving the dashboard.

## Calculated Dimensions and Measures
What it is: formula-based custom fields and metrics that update automatically with filters.
Use it for: paid-to-charge ratio, age bands, risk tiers, and other derived KPIs.

## Rich Interactive Visualizations
What it is: counters, bar, line, combo, scatter, pie, heatmaps, and point or choropleth maps.
Use it for: provider scorecards, facility maps, and complication-rate heatmaps that surface patterns and outliers.

## Multipage Reporting
What it is: structure one dashboard into multiple pages or tabs.
Use it for: tailored views such as Overview, Provider Performance, and Geography for different audiences.

## Parameterized Widgets
What it is: no-code dropdowns, date pickers, and inputs that change the underlying queries.
Use it for: a minimum-encounter threshold, a metric selector, or a date range, all self-service.

## Themes
What it is: governed colors, fonts, and layout for a consistent, branded look.
Use it for: a polished, on-brand dashboard when sharing with stakeholders or externally.

## Ask Genie
What it is: ask natural-language questions right inside the dashboard and see the generated SQL.
Use it for: instant, self-service answers like "readmissions by facility this quarter," with the SQL shown.

## Closing line (optional, bottom of page)
Everything here runs on governed data already in Databricks: no extracts, no separate BI server.
